digital platforms & the city

digital platforms are increasingly contributing to everyday urban experiences

social media is altering how society and the built environment are perceived

tiktok in particular positions videos that fit certain narratives and visual identities to go viral easily, altering how people engage with the city

digital platforms traditionally provide services like food delivery, navigation, ride-hailing, short-term rentalsâ€¦

these platforms produce social relations, symbolic hierarchies, modes of visibility, and ways of inhabiting a place. user-led content driven platforms employ locational user data in the form of reviews, images, and descriptions as method for commodifying urban place and transforming the fabric of city life.

through these elements, they are contributing to the increasing digitization and mediatization of the city. this constructs the symbolic economy of place, which refers to the production of space and symbols through culture.

platform gentrification

reconfiguring traditional forms of gentrification

from the construction of the symbolic economy of place, certain neighborhoods can become objects of desire in online spaces through videos, images, or posts that are circulated and reproduced online. defining and increasing (or decreasing) the desirability of a place online can change the value of a location, providing a means of increasing gentrification of a certain location.

this process is being understood as platform gentrification, which suggests that digital landscapes are producing spatial inequalities in the city, and are potentially incentivizing changes to investments in different locations.

research questions

  • which neighborhoods are being impacted by platform gentrification?
  • how do digital platforms shape the symbolic economy of place in nyc neighborhoods?
